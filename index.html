About me

My name is Guillaume and I've been currently living in Montreal for the last year. I graduated with a bachelor's degree in physics from the Université du Québec à Trois-Rivières in 2010, and a bachelor's degree in Physics Engineering from the Université Laval in 2013. I have been reorienting my career towards web development during the last 4 months, and attended DecodeMTL bootcamp in order to get to know the industry better.

I've been coding here and there during my college years, mostly in LaTeX, for typing scientific articles, and in Matlab, largely used by engineers and scientists to make simulations and numeric calculations. It's only up until recently that I came to the realization that back in my college years, the times I was most enjoying myself were when I was typing my scientific reports, or when I had to write some simulation scripts in Matlab, and that I might make a career out of this. When i'm not coding, I keep myself busy with quite a few other things, first one being self-learning. If I'm not on Youtube watching some tutorial on coding, I will be on Memrise learning Spanish, or on Youtube again watching some other tutorial about playing the guitar.

Hobbies and further projects

Along with being an amateur musician, I'm also a DIY electronician hobbyist. I design with DipTrace my own printed circuit boards, that I etch myself, and then assemble the whole circuit with a soldering iron. I also simulate some of my circuits with LtSpice in order to put some of my own modifications into them.

Professionally-wise, I beleive the next thing I want to learn is PHP. Even after being in use for more than 20 years, it still seems to be a trusty and relevant language to learn, and to be a good-to-have skill. Personnaly-wise, I would like to dig into Arduino in order to start to include some coding into my electronic activities, and when I learned that there are now some libraries such as Johnny-Five to code from NodeJs to Arduino, it already gave me one or two ideas of projects I would want to make.
